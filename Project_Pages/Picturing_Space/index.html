Picturing Space

Gallery for architectural photography: exploring mystery and discovery in the photograph and the journey

2011
72 K Street Boston, MA
6,550 sq. ft.

72 K Street is located in an area of Boston within walking distance to the well-known Institute for Contemporary Art. The building currently houses a woodworking studio and sign printers. Artists and designers are slowly moving into the neighborhood. This proposal is for a gallery focused on architectural photography.

This gallery celebrates the mystery, discovery, and voyeurism in photography. I looked at photographs that used techniques of layering and manipulation, and was especially inspired by double exposed images. Visitors must enter through a void in the middle of the gallery. The courtyard is divided from the gallery by walls of glass so that visitors can see some of the key pieces on view while still outside. Visibility of the entrance door is partially obscured by the main interior staircase, which cuts through the courtyard void and connects the upper and lower floors, that house the main and secondary galleries. As new visitors walk under this staircase to access the door, their paths intersect with those inside.
