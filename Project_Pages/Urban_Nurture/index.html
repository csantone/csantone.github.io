Urban Nurture

The container goes from shipping our food to growing our food
2010


Many countries import far more than they export, resulting in a surplus of shipping containers. These containers have too much embodied energy to be effectively recycled, which demands that we find a way to adapt containers in their current states. Urban-Nurture reuses shipping containers to help with a growing food shortage and reconnect us with our food.

The modules were designed to grow food efficiently and compactly in a 320-square-foot space through a layered aquaponics system. Yet re-purposed containers can do more than just grow food. They invite people inside to be surrounded by the sights (plants and fish), sounds (running water), and smells (vegetation) of gardening. They enliven abandoned and vacant spaces in neighborhoods. They have an innate portability and so can be temporary or permanent, isolated or grouped.

My original design for an aquaponics container was combined with Cheryle Cranbourne's design for a portable restroom to create the Urban-Nurture system.
