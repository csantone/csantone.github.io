Dinner for Theseus and Ariadne

2011
clay, colored slip, clear glaze
Plates measure approximately 8.5" in diameter

Dinner for Theseus and Ariadne tells a story as a meal is consumed. The story of Theseus and Ariadne was chosen because it inspires conversation between friends and strangers with its elements of travel, personal sacrifice, challenge, and unfulfilled love. The last two dishes, a salad bowl and quiche plate, were chosen to hold recipes that I often share with my friends.

Each piece was individually hand-thrown and trimmed on the potter's wheel. The drawings were carved and filled in with blue and black slip.

Every 9 years 14 children are sent from Athens to Crete as a sacrifice to the minotaur. Theseus, an Anthenian vows to kill the minotaur. Upon his arrival to Crete, Theseus and Princess Ariadne of Crete, fall in love. She gives him a sword and a ball of string to help him find his way through the minotaur's deadly labyrinth. Theseus locates the minotaur and kills him using Ariadne's sword. The ball of string leads him back through the labyrinth. He takes Ariadne with him on his return journey to Athens, but they are blown off-course and land on a deserted island. Theseus leaves the island the following morning, without Ariadne, who is asleep on the beach.

The end of the story is revealed as the salad and quiche are consumed.
